# Acceptable Use Policy

**Last Updated: [DATE]**

## Introduction

This Acceptable Use Policy ("AUP") defines prohibited content and activities when using [COMPANY_NAME]'s MultinotesAI platform and services (the "Services"). This AUP is incorporated into and forms part of our Terms of Service.

By using the Services, you agree to comply with this AUP. Violations may result in content removal, account suspension or termination, and potential legal action.

## 1. General Principles

You agree to use the Services in a manner that:

- **Complies with Laws**: Adheres to all applicable local, state, national, and international laws and regulations
- **Respects Rights**: Respects the intellectual property, privacy, and other rights of others
- **Promotes Safety**: Does not endanger, harm, or harass individuals or groups
- **Acts Responsibly**: Uses AI features ethically and responsibly
- **Operates Fairly**: Does not abuse, exploit, or circumvent the Services

## 2. Prohibited Content

You may not create, upload, store, share, or generate content through our AI features that:

### 2.1 Illegal Content

- **Criminal Activity**: Content that promotes, facilitates, or provides instructions for illegal activities, including:
  - Drug manufacturing, trafficking, or distribution
  - Weapons manufacturing (explosives, firearms, etc.)
  - Human trafficking or exploitation
  - Money laundering or fraud schemes
  - Identity theft or unauthorized access to systems
  - Piracy or circumvention of digital rights management

- **Regulated Goods**: Unlicensed sale or distribution of regulated items (pharmaceuticals, alcohol, tobacco, firearms)

- **Counterfeit Items**: Creation or distribution of counterfeit goods, currency, or documents

### 2.2 Harmful or Dangerous Content

- **Violence**: Content that:
  - Glorifies, incites, or threatens violence against individuals or groups
  - Provides detailed instructions for physical harm or self-harm
  - Depicts graphic violence or gore intended to shock or disturb
  - Promotes terrorism or violent extremism

- **Self-Harm**: Content that:
  - Encourages suicide or self-injury
  - Provides methods or instructions for self-harm
  - Glorifies eating disorders or dangerous behaviors

- **Dangerous Activities**: Content promoting dangerous challenges, stunts, or activities that risk serious injury

### 2.3 Hate Speech and Harassment

- **Hate Speech**: Content that attacks, threatens, or dehumanizes individuals or groups based on:
  - Race, ethnicity, or national origin
  - Religion or religious affiliation
  - Gender or gender identity
  - Sexual orientation
  - Disability or medical condition
  - Age
  - Caste or social status

- **Harassment and Bullying**: Content that:
  - Targets individuals for abuse, intimidation, or harassment
  - Encourages coordinated harassment campaigns
  - Shares personal information for harassment purposes (doxxing)
  - Creates or shares non-consensual intimate images
  - Engages in stalking or persistent unwanted contact

- **Threats**: Direct or implied threats of violence, harm, or intimidation

### 2.4 Adult and Sexual Content

- **Child Safety**: Absolutely no content involving:
  - Child sexual abuse material (CSAM)
  - Sexualization of minors
  - Grooming or solicitation of minors
  - Any content that endangers children

  **VIOLATIONS WILL BE REPORTED TO LAW ENFORCEMENT AND THE NATIONAL CENTER FOR MISSING & EXPLOITED CHILDREN (NCMEC).**

- **Adult Content**: Sexually explicit content, including:
  - Pornographic images, videos, or text
  - Sexually explicit AI-generated content
  - Graphic sexual descriptions
  - Adult services or escort services

- **Non-Consensual Content**: Intimate images or content shared without consent (revenge porn)

### 2.5 Fraudulent or Deceptive Content

- **Scams and Fraud**: Content that:
  - Perpetrates scams or fraudulent schemes
  - Impersonates individuals, organizations, or entities
  - Creates phishing materials or fake login pages
  - Spreads pyramid schemes or multi-level marketing scams

- **Misinformation**: Deliberately false or misleading content about:
  - Health emergencies or public health crises
  - Elections or voting processes
  - Natural disasters or emergencies
  - Financial markets (with intent to manipulate)

- **Fake Reviews**: Creating fraudulent reviews, testimonials, or endorsements

### 2.6 Spam and Malicious Content

- **Spam**: Unsolicited bulk messages, advertisements, or promotional content
- **Malware**: Viruses, trojans, ransomware, or other malicious code
- **Phishing**: Content designed to steal credentials or personal information
- **Exploits**: Security exploits or vulnerabilities intended for malicious purposes

### 2.7 Intellectual Property Violations

- **Copyright Infringement**: Unauthorized copying, distribution, or use of copyrighted materials
- **Trademark Violations**: Unauthorized use of trademarks or brand names
- **Plagiarism**: Passing off others' work as your own
- **Pirated Content**: Distribution of pirated software, media, or digital content

### 2.8 Privacy Violations

- **Personal Information**: Sharing others' personal information without consent, including:
  - Full names with contact information
  - Physical addresses or locations
  - Phone numbers or email addresses
  - Social security numbers, credit card numbers, or financial information
  - Medical records or health information
  - Private communications or documents

- **Surveillance**: Unauthorized tracking, monitoring, or surveillance of individuals

### 2.9 Discrimination and Exclusion

- **Discriminatory Content**: Content that discriminates against protected classes in:
  - Employment opportunities
  - Housing advertisements
  - Credit or financial services
  - Educational opportunities
  - Public accommodations

## 3. Prohibited Activities

You may not engage in activities that:

### 3.1 Abuse of Services

- **Circumventing Limits**: Attempts to bypass usage limits, rate limits, or access controls
- **Account Manipulation**: Creating multiple accounts to abuse free trials or evade bans
- **Automated Access**: Using bots, scrapers, or automated tools without authorization
- **Resource Abuse**: Excessive use that degrades service performance for others
- **Service Disruption**: Activities that disrupt, interfere with, or harm the Services

### 3.2 Security Violations

- **Unauthorized Access**: Attempting to:
  - Access accounts or data belonging to others
  - Bypass authentication or security measures
  - Probe, scan, or test vulnerabilities
  - Gain unauthorized access to our systems or infrastructure

- **Hacking**: Engaging in any form of:
  - Network penetration or intrusion
  - Password cracking or credential stuffing
  - Denial-of-service (DoS) or distributed denial-of-service (DDoS) attacks
  - SQL injection or code injection attacks

- **Data Extraction**: Unauthorized scraping, harvesting, or extraction of data

### 3.3 Commercial Abuse

- **Unauthorized Commercial Use**: Using free accounts for commercial purposes requiring paid plans
- **Reselling Services**: Reselling, sublicensing, or redistributing our Services without authorization
- **Competitive Analysis**: Using the Services to build competing products or services
- **Benchmarking**: Publishing benchmark tests without our written permission

### 3.4 Platform Manipulation

- **Gaming Systems**: Manipulating algorithms, rankings, or recommendation systems
- **False Engagement**: Creating fake engagement, likes, shares, or followers
- **Sock Puppets**: Operating multiple accounts to create false consensus or amplify messages
- **Vote Manipulation**: Manipulating voting systems or polls

### 3.5 Third-Party Terms Violations

- **API Violations**: Violating the terms of third-party APIs we use (OpenAI, Anthropic, etc.)
- **Integration Abuse**: Misusing integrations with third-party services
- **License Violations**: Violating open-source licenses or third-party software licenses

## 4. AI Usage Guidelines

When using our AI-powered features, you must:

### 4.1 Responsible AI Use

- **Verify Output**: Review and verify AI-generated content before use, especially for:
  - Critical decisions (medical, legal, financial)
  - Public statements or published content
  - Content that could cause harm if inaccurate

- **Understand Limitations**: Recognize that AI may:
  - Generate inaccurate, biased, or misleading information
  - Produce inconsistent or unpredictable results
  - Reflect biases present in training data
  - Create content that infringes third-party rights

- **Use Ethically**: Ensure AI-generated content aligns with ethical standards and does not:
  - Deceive or mislead users
  - Violate privacy or consent
  - Perpetuate harmful stereotypes or biases
  - Replace human judgment in critical contexts

### 4.2 Prohibited AI Uses

Do not use AI features to:

- **Generate Prohibited Content**: Create any content listed in Section 2 (Prohibited Content)
- **Impersonation**: Generate content impersonating real individuals without disclosure
- **Academic Dishonesty**: Complete assignments or exams in violation of academic integrity policies
- **Automated Decisions**: Make fully automated decisions affecting legal rights, safety, or well-being
- **Deepfakes**: Create realistic fake audio, video, or images without clear disclosure
- **Mass Manipulation**: Generate content for coordinated inauthentic behavior or manipulation campaigns
- **Unauthorized Professional Services**: Provide medical, legal, or financial advice without appropriate disclaimers

### 4.3 Attribution and Transparency

- **Disclose AI Use**: When appropriate, disclose that content was AI-generated, especially for:
  - Published articles or professional content
  - Academic or research work
  - Content that could mislead if thought to be human-created

- **Don't Claim Authorship**: Do not claim sole authorship of substantially AI-generated content without disclosure

### 4.4 Prompt Engineering Abuse

Do not engage in prompt injection, jailbreaking, or attempts to:

- Circumvent safety filters or content policies
- Extract training data or model parameters
- Cause the AI to violate its own usage policies
- Create prompts designed to produce harmful outputs

## 5. Consequences of Violations

Violations of this AUP may result in the following actions, at our sole discretion:

### 5.1 Warning

- First-time or minor violations may result in a warning
- We may provide guidance on how to comply with this AUP
- Repeated violations after warnings may result in escalated enforcement

### 5.2 Content Removal

- Immediate removal of violating content
- Notification of removal and reason (when appropriate)
- No compensation for removed content

### 5.3 Account Restrictions

- **Temporary Suspension**: Limited access for a specified period (e.g., 7-30 days)
- **Feature Restrictions**: Temporary or permanent restriction of specific features (e.g., AI capabilities, sharing)
- **Read-Only Access**: Ability to access and export data but not create new content

### 5.4 Account Termination

- **Permanent Ban**: Complete and permanent termination of account access
- **No Refunds**: No refunds for prepaid subscription fees
- **Data Deletion**: Content may be deleted immediately or after a short grace period

### 5.5 Legal Action

For serious violations, we may:

- Report illegal activity to law enforcement
- Report CSAM to NCMEC and appropriate authorities
- Cooperate with investigations and legal proceedings
- Pursue civil or criminal legal action
- Seek damages, injunctive relief, or other remedies

### 5.6 Factors Considered

When determining enforcement actions, we consider:

- Severity and nature of the violation
- Harm caused to individuals, groups, or the platform
- Intent (accidental vs. deliberate violations)
- User's history of compliance or violations
- Responsiveness to warnings or requests for correction
- Legal requirements and obligations

## 6. Reporting Violations
