## 2. Legal Basis for Processing

We process your personal data only when we have a legal basis under GDPR Article 6. The legal bases we rely on include:

### 2.1 Contract Performance (Article 6(1)(b))

Processing necessary to perform our contract with you:

- **Account Creation and Management**: Processing account information to provide Services
- **Service Delivery**: Processing content and usage data to deliver AI-powered features
- **Billing and Payments**: Processing payment information for subscription management
- **Customer Support**: Processing support requests and communications
- **Service Notifications**: Sending essential service updates and security alerts

### 2.2 Legitimate Interests (Article 6(1)(f))

Processing necessary for our legitimate interests (or those of a third party), provided your rights do not override:

- **Service Improvement**: Analyzing usage patterns to enhance features and performance
- **Security and Fraud Prevention**: Detecting and preventing security threats, abuse, and fraud
- **Network and Information Security**: Ensuring the security and integrity of our systems
- **Business Analytics**: Understanding business performance and user needs
- **Direct Marketing**: Sending promotional communications (where consent is not required)
- **Internal Administration**: Managing operations, records, and business processes

**Balancing Test**: We have assessed that these interests do not override your fundamental rights and freedoms. You have the right to object to processing based on legitimate interests.

### 2.3 Consent (Article 6(1)(a))

Processing based on your freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous consent:

- **Marketing Communications**: Promotional emails, newsletters (where consent is required by local law)
- **Optional Features**: Analytics cookies, personalization features, beta programs
- **Special Categories of Data**: Processing sensitive data (if applicable)
- **Third-Party Data Sharing**: Sharing with partners beyond what's necessary for service provision

**Withdrawal of Consent**: You may withdraw consent at any time through account settings or by contacting us. Withdrawal does not affect the lawfulness of processing before withdrawal.

### 2.4 Legal Obligation (Article 6(1)(c))

Processing necessary to comply with legal obligations:

- **Tax and Accounting**: Retaining financial records as required by law
- **Regulatory Compliance**: Responding to lawful requests from authorities
- **Law Enforcement**: Cooperating with investigations when legally required
- **Content Moderation**: Reporting illegal content (CSAM, terrorism) to authorities

### 2.5 Vital Interests (Article 6(1)(d))

Processing necessary to protect vital interests (rarely applicable):

- **Emergency Situations**: Preventing imminent harm to life or health
- **Crisis Response**: Responding to life-threatening emergencies

### 2.6 Public Interest (Article 6(1)(e))

We do not generally rely on this basis, but it may apply for:

- **Research**: Conducting research in the public interest (using anonymized data)
- **Archiving**: Preserving data for historical or scientific purposes

## 4. Data Subject Rights Under GDPR

As a data subject in the EU/EEA, you have the following rights:

### 4.1 Right of Access (Article 15)

You have the right to:

- Confirm whether we process your personal data
- Obtain a copy of your personal data
- Receive information about how we process your data

**How to Exercise**: Use the "Export Data" feature in account settings or contact privacy@[COMPANY_NAME].com

**Response Time**: Within 1 month (may be extended by 2 months for complex requests)

**Format**: Data provided in commonly used electronic format (JSON, CSV)

**Free of Charge**: First copy is free; additional copies may incur a reasonable fee

### 4.2 Right to Rectification (Article 16)

You have the right to:

- Correct inaccurate personal data
- Complete incomplete personal data

**How to Exercise**: Update information directly in account settings or contact support@[COMPANY_NAME].com

**Response Time**: Within 1 month

**Verification**: We may request verification before making changes to critical information

### 4.3 Right to Erasure / "Right to be Forgotten" (Article 17)

You have the right to request deletion of your personal data when:

- Data is no longer necessary for the purposes it was collected
- You withdraw consent (where processing is based on consent)
- You object to processing based on legitimate interests
- Data has been unlawfully processed
- Erasure is required by legal obligation
- Data was collected in relation to information society services offered to children

**How to Exercise**: Use the "Delete Account" feature in account settings or contact privacy@[COMPANY_NAME].com

**Response Time**: Within 1 month; data deleted within 30 days of confirmation

**Exceptions**: We may retain data if necessary for:
- Compliance with legal obligations
- Establishment, exercise, or defense of legal claims
- Archiving purposes in the public interest
- Freedom of expression and information

**Backup Retention**: Deleted data may remain in backups for up to 90 days

### 4.4 Right to Restriction of Processing (Article 18)

You have the right to request restriction (rather than deletion) when:

- You contest the accuracy of data (while we verify)
- Processing is unlawful, but you prefer restriction over deletion
- We no longer need the data, but you need it for legal claims
- You have objected to processing based on legitimate interests (pending verification)

**How to Exercise**: Contact privacy@[COMPANY_NAME].com

**Effect**: We will store but not process restricted data (except with your consent, for legal claims, or to protect others' rights)

**Notification**: We will inform you before lifting restriction

### 4.5 Right to Data Portability (Article 20)

You have the right to:

- Receive personal data you provided in a structured, machine-readable format
- Transmit that data to another controller without hindrance

**Scope**: Applies only to:
- Data you provided to us (not derived or inferred data)
- Processing based on consent or contract
- Automated processing

**How to Exercise**: Use the "Export Data" feature in account settings

**Format**: JSON, CSV, or other commonly used formats

**Direct Transfer**: We can transmit directly to another provider where technically feasible

**Not Applicable To**: Data derived from your input (analytics, AI-generated insights based on your usage patterns)

### 4.6 Right to Object (Article 21)

You have the right to object to processing based on:

#### 4.6.1 Legitimate Interests
- **Absolute Right**: For direct marketing purposes (we will stop immediately)
- **Conditional Right**: For other legitimate interest processing (we will stop unless we demonstrate compelling grounds)

**How to Exercise**: Use opt-out links in emails or contact privacy@[COMPANY_NAME].com

#### 4.6.2 Public Interest or Official Authority
- Right to object to processing necessary for public interest or official authority (rarely applicable)

#### 4.6.3 Profiling and Automated Decision-Making
- Right to object to automated decision-making with legal or similarly significant effects

**How to Exercise**: Contact privacy@[COMPANY_NAME].com

### 4.7 Rights Related to Automated Decision-Making (Article 22)

## 8. Security Measures

We implement technical and organizational measures to protect personal data (Article 32):

### 8.1 Technical Measures

- **Encryption**: TLS 1.2+ in transit, AES-256 at rest
- **Access Controls**: Role-based access, principle of least privilege
- **Authentication**: Multi-factor authentication, strong password requirements
- **Network Security**: Firewalls, intrusion detection, DDoS protection
- **Logging and Monitoring**: Security event logging and real-time monitoring
- **Vulnerability Management**: Regular security assessments and patching
- **Secure Development**: Security-by-design principles, code reviews

### 8.2 Organizational Measures

- **Employee Training**: Regular data protection and security training
- **Background Checks**: Screening of employees with data access
- **Confidentiality Obligations**: Contractual confidentiality for all personnel
- **Access Authorization**: Formal processes for granting and revoking access
- **Incident Response**: Documented procedures for data breaches
- **Business Continuity**: Disaster recovery and backup procedures
- **Vendor Management**: Due diligence and DPAs with processors

### 8.3 Data Breach Notification

In the event of a personal data breach:

**To Supervisory Authority** (Article 33):
- Notification within 72 hours of becoming aware (if risk to rights and freedoms)
- Details: nature, categories and number affected, consequences, measures taken

**To Data Subjects** (Article 34):
- Notification without undue delay (if high risk to rights and freedoms)
- Clear and plain language
- Recommendations for mitigation

**Your Rights**: You can contact dpo@[COMPANY_NAME].com for information about breaches affecting you.
